Before deciding to make up your own opinions on the Brave browser, I encourage you to do your own research and not take it from this one website alone.

## 2016 — ad replacement

brave promised to strip banner ads from websites and replace them with their own — extracting money from publishers without their knowledge or consent. 17 newspaper publishers sent a cease-and-desist.

[computerworld](https://www.computerworld.com/article/1697409/the-brave-browser-basics-what-it-does-how-it-differs-from-rivals.html) · [ubergizmo (cease-and-desist)](https://www.ubergizmo.com/2016/04/publishers-not-happy-brave-browser/) · [wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2016 — wikipedia clone in search

CEO brendan eich unilaterally added a fringe, pay-to-win wikipedia clone into the default search engine list.

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2016 — link bubble acquired then killed

brave acquired link bubble, an android browser, then separated and eventually terminated it in 2017 — eliminating a competing product after purchasing it.

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2018 — unauthorized donation solicitation

tom scott and other creators discovered brave was soliciting donations in their names without their knowledge or consent — and keeping the money if creators hadn't signed up to claim it.

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>) · [cpo magazine](https://www.cpomagazine.com/data-privacy/brave-privacy-browser-caught-automatically-adding-affiliate-links-to-cryptocurrency-urls/)

## 2019 — taunting competing browser users

brave ran ads on softonic.com that detected your browser and inserted its name directly into an accusatory message: _"firefox knows far more about you than just your location."_

[bleepingcomputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/software/brave-browser-taunts-chrome-edge-and-firefox-in-new-privacy-ad/)

## 2020 — affiliate link injection

brave was caught injecting affiliate codes into URLs when users navigated to binance, coinbase, ledger, and trezor — quietly earning referral commissions without disclosure. CEO brendan eich initially defended the practice before issuing an apology.

[david gerard](https://davidgerard.co.uk/blockchain/2020/06/06/the-brave-web-browser-is-hijacking-links-and-inserting-affiliate-codes/) · [android police](https://www.androidpolice.com/2020/06/07/brave-browser-caught-adding-its-own-referral-codes-to-some-cryptcurrency-trading-sites/) · [coindesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2020/06/08/brave-browsers-affiliate-link-controversy-explained)

## 2020 — silent ad injection on homepage

brave silently began injecting sponsored ads into new tab page backgrounds and pocketed the revenue. feedback on their own community: _"the sponsored backgrounds give a bad first impression."_

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2021 — tor DNS leak

brave's private window with tor was found leaking DNS queries — exposing the onion addresses users were visiting to their ISP and DNS provider. the vulnerability leaked for 91 days in the stable build. a patch was only deployed broadly after journalists published articles calling them out.

[bleepingcomputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/brave-privacy-bug-exposes-tor-onion-urls-to-your-dns-provider/) · [coindesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2021/02/22/brave-browser-was-exposing-addresses-in-tor-mode-for-months) · [the register](https://www.theregister.com/2021/02/22/in_brief_security/) · [techradar](https://www.techradar.com/news/brave-browser-found-to-leak-users-tor-dark-web-activity)

## 2022 — discouraging opt-out

brave floated the idea of actively discouraging users from disabling sponsored messages.

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2023 — VPN installed without consent

brave was caught silently installing a paid VPN service (brave firewall + VPN) on windows computers since april 2023 without user knowledge or consent. the VP of engineering acknowledged the issue on github only after public reports surfaced.

[ghacks](https://www.ghacks.net/2023/10/18/brave-is-installing-vpn-services-without-user-consent/) · [android central](https://www.androidcentral.com/apps-software/brave-browser-secretly-installing-vpn) · [windows central](https://www.windowscentral.com/software-apps/browsing/brave-browser-is-installing-a-vpn-without-your-permission) · [techradar](https://www.techradar.com/pro/vpn/brave-browser-under-fire-for-installing-its-vpn-without-user-permission)

## 2023 — covert data scraping and resale

brave's search crawler was designed specifically not to announce itself to website owners — masquerading as googlebot — while scraping and selling the crawled data. site operators had no way to selectively opt out.

[stackdiary (original report)](https://stackdiary.com/brave-selling-copyrighted-data-for-ai-training/) · [gigazine](https://gigazine.net/gsc_news/en/20230718-brave-selling-copyrighted-data/) · [hacker news discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=36735777) · [brave's own docs (admission)](https://search.brave.com/help/brave-search-crawler)

## 2024 — fingerprint protection quietly abandoned

brave gave up on advanced fingerprint protection, citing internal statistics that users who enabled protection were also likely to disable telemetry — making it inconvenient for brave's own data collection.

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>)

## 2025 — undisclosed conflict of interest

brave staff published an article endorsing privacytests.org as a "legitimate testing site" they "work with." the article failed to disclose that privacytests.org is run by arthur edelstein — a brave senior architect. the conflict had been reported as a github issue on the site since 2023.

[github conflict of interest report](https://github.com/privacytests/privacytests.org/issues/166)

## 2025 — "forget the fox" play store stunt

brave briefly renamed itself _"brave browser – forget the fox"_ in google play store search results for firefox. luke mulks, VP of business operations, publicly claimed it was a photoshopped meme — despite multiple independent screenshots proving it real. brave then quietly removed it.

[techissuestoday (full breakdown)](https://techissuestoday.com/the-firefox-vs-brave-browser-drama-explained/)

## misc — other noted behavior

- partnered with newegg to ship ads in physical boxes
- purchased link bubble (2016) then shut it down as standalone (2017)
- in 2019, ran targeted ads mocking users of chrome, edge, and firefox by name

[wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)>) · [bleepingcomputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/software/brave-browser-taunts-chrome-edge-and-firefox-in-new-privacy-ad/)
